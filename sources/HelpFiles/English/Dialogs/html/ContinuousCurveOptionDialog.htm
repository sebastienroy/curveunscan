Advanced options

Threshold color

The threshold color is the color used to differentiate the points belonging to the curve from the points belonging to the background of the graphic. If one of the components (red, green or blue) of a pixel is brighter than the threshold color one, the pixel is considered as belonging to the background.

Each component of the threshold color can be adjusted using either a slider or directly in the related edit field. The value must be between 0 (darker) and 255 (brighter).

The threshold color can also be chosen from the graphic, using the "Pick from bitmap" button.

Curve thickness compensation

The line thickness of the curve can be compensated through two different algorithms : the "basic" and the "smart" one.

The basic algorithm moves the points of the curve by a fixed value from top to bottom or from bottom to top, depending on the followed side. The value is the half of the curve thickness. The apparent compensation is different on the flat parts of the curve than on the sharp parts.

The "smart" algorithm adjusts the compensation value depending on the curve rate. This can lead to a good compensation at the same time on the flat parts and on the sharp parts of the curve. The number of points used to calculate the rate of the curve can be adjusted from 1 to 32. Attention, high values can lead to a bad compensation on complex curves, due to the "inertia" of the calculus.
